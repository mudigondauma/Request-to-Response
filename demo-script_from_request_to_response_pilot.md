# 3-Minute Demo Script — From Request to Response Pilot

## Opening (20 seconds)
"This is a working thin-slice prototype for the Agentic Solutioning System pilot. It is intentionally narrow: internal-only, read-only, draft-only, and human-reviewed. The goal is to prove that we can turn an ambiguous opportunity signal into a faster, more structured, and more governable first-pass response pack."

## Step 1 — Intake (35 seconds)
1. Open the **Intake** tab.
2. Click **Managed network transformation RFP**.
3. Point out:
   - approved inputs only
   - explicit constraints / risks
   - success criteria
   - completion bar
   - likely route preview

Say:
"This matters because the front door is structured. We are not asking the model to be clever with a vague prompt. We are forcing the work into a reviewable request shape."

## Step 2 — Response workbench (45 seconds)
1. Click **Generate governed response pack**.
2. Move to **Response workbench**.
3. Highlight:
   - opportunity summary
   - recommended solution patterns
   - assumptions and dependencies
   - delivery and governance risks
   - executive briefing draft
   - evidence trail

Say:
"The output is not generic chat. It is a structured internal pack that makes reasoning visible and links the draft back to approved internal sources."

## Step 3 — Human review (40 seconds)
1. Move to **Human review**.
2. Explain the Green / Amber / Red model.
3. Show why the example routes to **Amber**.
4. Click **Approve draft for internal use** or **Place hold / escalate**.

Say:
"This is the key control point. The pilot does not rely on trust in the model. It relies on bounded routing, review, and accountability."

## Step 4 — KPI cockpit (35 seconds)
1. Move to **KPI cockpit**.
2. Point out the balanced scorecard:
   - time to first structured pack
   - source traceability
   - unsupported claim rate
   - approved pattern reuse
3. Mention weekly ops review and threshold-based action.

Say:
"This is how the pilot becomes scale-ready. We measure speed, but we also measure whether governance and quality stay intact."

## Step 5 — Architecture and controls (25 seconds)
1. Move to **Architecture & controls**.
2. Show the multi-agent flow and the pilot guardrails.

Say:
"This makes the operating model explicit: where the agents help, where the human stays accountable, and what we are deliberately not automating yet."

## Close (20 seconds)
"So the pilot claim is modest but defensible: one narrow deal-shaping workflow can be accelerated with evidence, review gates, and measurable control before we invest in full integration."
